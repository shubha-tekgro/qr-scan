Patan Durbar Square

A living canvas of Newar craftsmanship, temples, and timeless stone.

A brief story

Patan Durbar Square, in the heart of Lalitpur, is a masterpiece of Newar architecture and craftsmanship. For centuries, its palaces, courtyards and temples have been a centre of artistic expression where wood, stone, and metal were used with exquisite skill.

The square grew around the royal palace of the Malla kings and became a hub for artisans who created intricate carvings, finely cast bronze sculptures, and delicate lattice windows. Each courtyard tells a story — of devotion, trade, and evolving styles.

Local festivals, rituals and daily life continue to animate the space. Bells chime in the breeze; craftsmen still carve and polish; and visitors are invited to listen closely and learn how built heritage holds the memory of a community.

Quick facts

  • Founded: Centuries — flourished under Malla kings
  • Style: Newar architecture, tiered temples, intricate woodwork
  • Why visit: Live craftsmanship, monuments & cultural festivals
